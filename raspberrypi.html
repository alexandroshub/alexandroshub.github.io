What is the Raspberry Pi?

The Raspberry Pi is a series of credit card sized single-board computers developed in the United Kingdom by the Raspberry Pi Foundation. It was originally designed to promote teaching of basic computer science in schools and in developing countries. The original model, costing around $35, became far more popular than anticipated, selling outside it's target market.
By February 2019, sales of all models reached 25 million, making it the best-selling British computer and the third best-selling general purpose computer after the PC and Mac.

"We'd hoped we might sell about 10,000 in the first year - so far it's been 400,000."

- Eben Upton, a founder of the Raspberry Pi Foundation speaking in 2012

Foundation

The Raspberry Pi Foundation was founded in May 2009 in Cambridge, UK.


Where is the Raspberry Pi Foundation located?

In Cambridge, United Kingdom (UK)


Who founded the Raspberry Pi Foundation?

The main founders are Eben Upton, Rob Mullins, Jack Lang, Alan Mycroft, Pete Lomas, and David Braben.


What is the mission of the Raspberry Pi Foundation?

To promote computer science education and digital skills for young people and learners worldwide, providing low-cost, accessible computing solutions.


Is the Raspberry Pi Foundation a non-profit organization?

Yes, it is a charity / non-profit organization.


How does the Raspberry Pi Foundation support education?

Through:

  • Affordable hardware (Raspberry Pi computers)
  • Educational resources, tutorials, and coding clubs (like CoderDojo and Raspberry Pi Clubs)
  • Teacher training programs
  • Online learning platforms and free learning materials

What projects or programs does the Raspberry Pi Foundation run?
  • Raspberry Pi Projects & Resources for learning coding, electronics, and digital making
  • CoderDojo clubs for kids
  • Raspberry Pi Certified Educator program
  • Community events like Raspberry Jams

Can anyone join or contribute to the Raspberry Pi Foundation?

Yes, individuals can support, donate, or participate in community projects, attend clubs, or contribute to educational content.


How is the Raspberry Pi Foundation funded?

Through sales of Raspberry Pi computers, donations, grants, and educational partnerships.


When was the first Raspberry Pi released?

The first Raspberry Pi was released on the 29th of February, 2012. This was the Raspberry Pi 1 Model B. The Model A of the Raspberry Pi 1 was released on the 4th of February, 2013.

Naming

The name is a play on words; raspberry pie (homophonic with the English pronunciation of Pi) means "raspberry tart" or "raspberry cake" in English. "Raspberry" connects to the tradition of naming computers after fruits, such as Apple or Acorn;[9] on the other hand, "Raspberry" has a derogatory connotation in English, similar to "cucumber" or "lemon" in German, and refers to the computer's low price and (compared to PCs of its time) low performance. "Pi" stands for "Python interpreter." Originally, the computer was intended to be delivered with a built-in interpreter for the Python programming language, similar to how almost all home computers of the 1980s had a built-in BASIC interpreter.[10]

Operating System (OS)

Raspberry Pi OS is the official operating system developed by the Raspberry Pi Foundation for Raspberry Pi computers. It is a Linux-based distribution derived from Debian, optimized for the Piâ€™s ARM architecture. The OS comes in multiple versions, including a full desktop environment, lightweight versions, and minimal images for advanced users. It includes pre-installed educational and programming tools, such as Python, Scratch, and development utilities, making it ideal for learning, coding, and general computing on Raspberry Pi devices. Raspberry Pi OS is designed to be lightweight, efficient, and user-friendly, supporting both beginners and experienced users.
